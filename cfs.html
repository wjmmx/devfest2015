2016 RSG HANGZHOU Program Committee emphasizes Practicableness, Feasibility and Innovation in program track design, and want to explore more novel interaction modes besides traditional mode where lecturers deliver and audiences receive, to enhance the learning and communication effectiveness. With all these considerations, the final track series presented here, are aligned with different roles, objectives and activities in agile practices, and reflect a path behind “learning-doing-Being” theme. Besides regular speech session, 2016 RSG HANGZHOU also includes innovative formats including workshop, posters and Pecha Kucha, to improve the interaction and communication between lecturer and audience.
Submission Format and Speaker Benefit
Below is the list of tracks of this gathering and a brief description about their targeted content. Besides Pecha Kucha, each track accepts submissions in different formats like speech session, workshop, poster, etc.

LEAN Startup and Product Innovation

Creating customer value and satisfaction are at the very heart of modern marketing thinking and practice. What are the winning strategies and how to develop them to operate within this very dynamic and rapid changing global environment are the questions in front of every company. No matter it is an enterprise or a Startup.
We expect to see submissions about how to use concepts, tools and mechanisms in agile and LEAN startup to help production innovation, business expansion and ultimately realizing customer value. Based on such expectation, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • LEAN startup
  • Customer collaboration and business value creation
  • Product Innovation and Effective Product Development (User Story Mapping etc.)

    • Agile Leadership and Coaching Techniques

    On the way of agile adoption, many organizations found they are already quite good at the nuts and bolts of agile team delivery. But focusing on team delivery alone is not enough. [Inability to change organizational culture] and [general resistance to change] are the two largest barriers. No matter which cap (Scrum Master, Coach, Manager, CEO) you are wearing, the mindset shifting, the corresponding new skills and practices at the management and the organizational level to create an agile environment are crucial.
    This track expects to see submissions about improving agile leadership and coaching ability, and ultimately benefit agile implementation and realize customer value, it can be experiences, lessons learned, innovation, exploration and challenges in related areas. Based on such expectation, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • Agile mindset shifting
  • Agile Leadership Frameworks
  • Agile Approaches to Change
  • Essential Agile coaching techniques
  • Agile coaching success or failure modes

    • Organization Transformation and Large Scale Agile

    Agile roots from small daily practices but it has been proven in many large organizations to improve their productivity and efficiency. There have been both successful and failed cases in different industries, different organization cultures and different support levels from senior management. There is always an enthusiasm to figure out the very reasons behind.
    This track focuses on how to improve organization’s productivity and efficiency using agile and scrum, especially for large organizations’ agile and scrum implementation. We hope to see innovative solutions, and (hope more) to see failure cases and lessons learned. Based on such expectation, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • Agile adoption in large corporations (with 500+ employees)
  • Agile transformation in multi-national team (with spread across multiple time-zones)
  • Agile adoption in non-IT industry
  • Grow Feature Team

  • Self-organized team building
  • Team collaboration and conflict resolving
  • Team Culture
  • Stakeholder management
  • Collaboration model
  • Continuous improvement and R&D productivity improvement

    • Agile Activities – Design and Architecture

    Agile software development prefers move fast and move small, but architecture and design needs to look beyond current demand to consider future scalability to meet business pace. Good architecture can greatly improve team’s productivity, and provide smooth and reliable support for business growth. How to achieve this, balance between “short term” and “long term” considerations on design and architecture is always the key.
    This track expects to see explorations and discussions about design and architect activities under an “agile” context. Are there differences in design activities between agile and waterfall model? Is there any excellence framework derived from agile activities, and provide great support to improve agile teams’ productivity later on? With these questions, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • Design and architecture under agile development
  • Transform design and architecture from waterfall to agile
  • Good design and architecture supported agile development
  • Good design and architecture fitting agile development

    • Agile Activities – Software Craftsmanship

    Software engineers call themselves “craftsman” more and more. Different industries have different business demands and different technical challenges, but software engineers pursue how to improve their “skills” has been a long lasting demand. One characteristic of agile is small steps but move fast. How could an engineer improve his skills continuously even under a fast moving iteration pace, what kind of challenges different “styles” of programmers may face under agile context, what kind of techniques you have to make you and your team works more efficiently. With these questions, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • GOOS/ TDD/ ATDD
  • Team Swarm
  • Functional Programming
  • HTML5/ Scala/ Clojure
  • Database Programming
  • Script Programming
  • Code Metrics and Analysis

    • Agile Activities – Quality Assurance

    The small steps and continuous improvement working model in agile development brings big impact against Quality Assurance activity in traditional software R&D. Agile development requires quality assurance improvement to ensure delivery effectiveness even under a fast pace, requires enrichments in quality assurance toolkit to support different testing works in agile context, requires quality assurance to provide measurement and realize process transparency and measurable improvement, requires quality assurance workers to improve skill sets and be part of a “feature team”. With these questions, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • Software measurement in agile development
  • Explorative testing in agile development
  • Performance testing and non-functional testing in agile development
  • Quality assurance and quality control in agile development
  • Quality assurance in Feature team
  • QA individual development in agile development

    • Agile Activities – DEVOPS and System Engineering

    Agile development breaks barriers between business and IT, and between different SDLC roles within IT department, and improve delivery efficiency greatly. However, there is still an invisible wall between R&D team and Operation team (or system engineering team), which prevents the completion of a full continuous improvement circle within big IT team. The rising of DEVOPS is the best ever choice to break this invisible wall. DEVOPS concept comes down in one continuous line with continuous integration and continuous delivery. It proposes the setup of a continuous feedback and improvement mechanism between R&D and Operation team to improve delivery quality, production stability and save operational cost.
    This track expects to see exercises and case studies from first line agile teams about DEVOPS and system engineering. We expects to see discussions about when DEVOPS is integrated into agile development, what kind of changes will happen within working model in IT, collaboration model between business and IT, and the approach to buy, construct, deliver and manage software systems. With these expectations, the submissions can be related but not limited to below:
  • Best practice in agile development regarding system engineering
  • DEVOPS migration for legacy applications
  • Continuous integration and continuous delivery
  • Release engineering
  • Ops oriented agile development, design and testing
  • Agility in operations
  • API driven operations
